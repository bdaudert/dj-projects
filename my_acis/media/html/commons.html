What is the California Climate Tracker?

An interactive web-based interface that can be used to understand temperature and precipitation variations over 11 distinct climate regions in California.

What dataset is being used?

Monthly station data, taken from cooperative observers (COOP), along with gridded data from the PRISM database at 4km resolution, are used to assess climate across the state.

What is the North American Freezing Level Tracker?

An interactive web-based interface that allows one to track through time the height of the freezing level (0 C or 32 F) above sea level.

What dataset is being used?

Values are based on the NCEP/NCAR Global Reanalysis determined every six hours from January 1948 until the last few days. The grid spacing is approximately 2.5 x 2.5 degrees of latitude/longitude. Values are interpolated to the latitude/longitude selected by the user.

What can you do with this tool?

Monthly and Seasonal time series plots of Freezing levels

Monthly and Seasonal time series plots of % of Precipitation as Snow

Yearly Summary plots

View and Download data

What is Westmap?

An interactive web-based interface that can be used to understand climate variability.

What dataset is being used?

The initial fundamental data is monthly PRISM temperature (maximum, mean, minimum) and precipitation at 4km resolution. Period of record is 1895-present. Currently, WestMap supports gridded PRISM climate data for the Western United States.

What can you do with this tool?

Generate Composite and Difference Maps

Create Time Series Plots

Compute basic statistics

Download Data, Maps, Plots

Access metadata

What is the Metadata Graphics Tool?

An interactive web-based interface that can be used to investigate availability of daily data for the period of record of a station in the COOP network for the 5 most common climate elements: Maximum Temperature, Minimum Temperature, Total Precipitation, Total Snowfall, Total Snowdepth.

What dataset is being used?

This tool works with COOP station data found in the ACIS system.
