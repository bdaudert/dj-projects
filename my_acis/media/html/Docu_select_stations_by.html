Select stations by
Type of search area. Map Interfaces will help you find the identifiers or enable you to draw a custom polygon (circles and bounding boxes included). Several search area options are available.
  • Individual station: Search by name or identifier of a single station.
  • List of stations: Search by names or identifiers of multiple stations.
  • State: Search by US state abbreviation (not case sensitive).
  • County: Search by US County FIPS code or name.
  • County Warning Area: Search by US County Warning Area code or name.
  • Climate Division: Search by US Climate Division code or name.
  • Basin: Search by US Basin code or name.
  • Custom Shape: Search by longitude, latitude coordinates or use the map interface to draw a custom polygon.
  • Using the map interface to find County/County Warning Area/Climate Division/Basin codes
    • Chose the US state of interest.
    • Click Update Map.
    • Once the map overlays are visible, click on an area on the map to see the name and id of that area.
    • Note that clicking on an area on the map will populate the main form with the coresponding identifier.
  • Using the map interface to draw a custom shape
    • You can place a marker on the map, draw a connected line segment, rectangle, circle or polygon. Click on the appropriate icon in the legend on the top of the map first.
    • Marker: Point and click on the map.
    • Connected line segment: Point and click at each junction point of your line. Finish by double clicking at the final point.
    • Rectangle: Point at a corner of your rectangle, click, hold and drag in the desired direction.
    • Circle: Point at the center of your circle, click, hold and drag outwards.
    • Polygon: Point and click at each corner of your polygon. Finish by double clicking at the final point or by clicking on the first point of your ploygon.
