Weather-Bureau-Army-Navy
Weather Bureau Army Navy network. WBAN identifiers were an effort to standardize meteorological identifiers in the 1950s and 1960s. Five-digit WBAN identifiers generally, but not exclusively, apply to aviation facilities. Stations in this network likely have an ICAO, FAA, and/or NWSLI indicator and may also have a GHCN-D, WMO, or COOP identifier as well.

Cooperative Observer Program
National Weather Service Cooperative Observer network. Volunteer observers make daily observations for this network at stations throughout the US and its territories. Observations in the COOP network begin as early as the mid- to late-1800s. Stations in this network may also have a GHCN-D, ICAO, NWSLI, FAA, WMO, or WBAN identifier.

Federal Aviation Administration
Federal Aviation Administration network. FAA codes are 3- or 4- digit alphanumeric codes used to identify aviation facilities. Stations in this network may also have a GHCN-D, ICAO, COOP, NWSLI, WMO, or WBAN identifier.

World Meteorological Organization
World Meteorological Organization network. WMO established 5-digit numeric codes to create a common identifier format for weather stations around the world. Stations in this network may also have a GHCN-D, ICAO, NWSLI, FAA, COOP, or WBAN identifier.

International Civil Aviation Organization
International Civil Aviation Administration network. ICAO identifiers are 4-letter codes used to identify the location of aviation facilities. Stations in this network likely have an FAA, WBAN, and/or NWSLI indicator and may also have a GHCN-D, WMO, or COOP identifier as well.

GHCN-Daily
Global Historic Climatology Network-Daily. A database of daily observations from numerous sources around the world that have been subject to a common suite of quality assurance reviews. Observations in this network begin as early as the mid- to late- 1800s. Stations in this network may also have a COOP, ICAO, NWSLI, FAA, WMO, or WBAN identifier.

National Weather Service Location Identifier
National Weather Service Location Identifier. A 5-digit alphanumeric code assigned to stations by the NWS and generally used for internal purposes. Stations in this network may also have a GHCN-D, ICAO, COOP, FAA, WMO, or WBAN identifier.

SNOTEL-Daily SNOwpack TELemetry stations operated by the Natural Resources Conservation Service. SNOTEL stations were first installed in the late 70s. Sub-daily observations made at SNOTEL stations are aggregated into daily observations of mean, max, and min temperature and precipitation to create the SNOTEL-Daily dataset. These daily data are part of the GHCN-D database.

ThreadEx
Climatological records where data from two or more station records are “threaded” together to create one continuous, long-term record for a location, typically in major metropolitan areas. Threadex records are built from GHCN-D records and exist for 270 locations in the US.

Community Collaborative Rain, Hail and Snow Network
Community Collaborative Rain, Hail, and Snow network. Network of volunteer observers throughout the US who make daily precipitation observations. Records begin as early as 1998. Anyone can join the network by visiting cocorahs.org.

RAWS-Daily Remote Automatic Weather Stations operated by the National Interagency Fire Center. RAWS stations were first installed in early 1980s. Sub-daily observations made at RAWS stations are aggregated into daily observations of mean, max, and min temperature to create the RAWS-Daily dataset. These daily data are part of the GHCN-D database.
