Oauth2 Instructions
Once you have set up your account, you can view your credentials (Account ID, email, keys) at the Console
Note that it is advantageous to add the Google EE account information to your environment variables:
In your .bashrc (.bash_profile on OSX), add these lines (xxxxxxx is your account number)


    #Google Earth Engine env
    export EE_SERVICE_ACCOUNT=xxxxxxx.apps.googleusercontent.com
    export EE_SERVICE_EMAIL= xxxxxxx@developer.gserviceaccount.com
    export EE_PRIVATE_KEY_FILE=/path_to_.p12_file

General guidelines and tips from Dr. Britta

localhost versus appspot

Just because your code runs on your localhost does not mean it will run on appspot This may be due to appspot not being able to locate one or more of the packages you are using.

Error messages and logs

If your browser and your developer console do not give you constructive error message, go to the Console and check the logs under the projects Monitoring tab on the left.

Effect of code changes

On localhost it is sufficient to reload your browser for code changes to take effect. For code changes to take effect on appspot, you will need to redeploy your code.
