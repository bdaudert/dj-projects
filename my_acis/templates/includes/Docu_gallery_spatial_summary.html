What is the application?

{{app_names.spatial_summary}}
You can use this tool to generate time series of climate variables
averaged over an area of interest.

What types of questions can be ansered?

  • What was the average, median, min, max or sum of
    temperature, precipitation, snow, degree day ... in a
    state, county, climate division, county warning area or custo shape
    over a custom date range.

Example 1

The appropriate timing of flowering of plants is crucial for plant reproduction.
Plants have developed mechanisms to detect environmental signals such as
carbohydrate status, photoperiod and temperature.
Crops are particularly sensitive to to temperature.
Climate change and the flowering time of annual crops
Presume you are a farmer in the Central California Valley.
Your crop usually start blooming in February.
This year, you have observed an early onset of flowering in January.
You would like to determine if this is due to warmer than normal temperatures in your area
or if other factors have played a role. January max, min and mean temperatures on average are
56F, 37F, 46F, respectively in yur area.
Let's see how these numbers compare to this year.

Step by Step instuctions

  1. Go to {{app_names.spatial_summary}}
  2. Set the form parameters:
    Since we are looking at a local farm we want to choose custom shape


    Use the map interface to draw a custom polygon, rectangle or circle around your farm.
    • Using the map interface to draw a custom shape
      • You can place a marker on the map or
        draw a rectangle, circle or polygon.
        Click on the appropriate icon in the legend on the top of the map.
      • Marker: Point and click on the map.
      • Rectangle: Point at a corner of your rectangle,
        click, hold and drag in the desired direction.
      • Circle: Point at the center of your circle,
        click, hold and drag outwards.
      • Polygon: Point and click at each corner of your polygon.
        Finish by double clicking at the final point
        or by clicking on the first point of your ploygon.
      Note that once you finished drawing you custom shape,
      form field "Custom Shape" on the left was updated with the coordinates of your polygon.
      Because we are looking at a smaller area that may not contain any weather stations,
      we should probably work with Data Type: gridded data.
      Pick Maximum, Minimum and Average Temperature as you elements.
      To select multiple elements:
      • MAC: hold down the command (apple) button while selecting the elements.
      • PC: hold down the control (ctrl) button while selecting the elements.
      We are only interested in January temperatures so pick
      Start Date: 2015-01-01, End Date: 2015-01-31

You can look at each element individually or look at multiple elements

By looking at the plots fro max, min and mean emperatures, we see that
Average max temperature in January lies at 59F
Average min temperature in January lies at 38F and
Average mean temperature in January lies at 48.5F
That is somewhat higher than the average temperatures( 56F, 37F, 46F)
recorded in this area in the past.
Depending on the sensitivity of your crop's blooming to temperature
you now have a better idea if temperature causeed this year's early onset.
