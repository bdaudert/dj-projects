Overview

SCENIC provides ACCESS TO CLIMATE DATA AND ANALYSIS TOOLS to support the research and decision making efforts of the Southwest Climate Science Center (SW CSC) and the Landscape Conservation Cooperatives(LCC).


In 2009 the US Department of Interior initiated a national system of Climate Science Centers (CSCs). This is the first-ever coordinated effort to synthesize existing climate change information to inform decisions about resource management. The Southwest CSC (SW CSC) consists of six partner institutions, one of which (Desert Research Institute) is the host of the Western Regional Climate center (WRCC). As part of its contribution to the SW CSC, WRCC is developing an access point to historic and real-time climate data tools and applications for decision support as well as meaningful access to other links and resources pertaining to climate and climate change in the Southwest US.

What We Do

The Southwest Climate and ENvironmental Information Collaborative (SCENIC) serves as an access point of climate information for the full extent or any subset of the Southwest US. Users can download various climate datasets and utilize our custom build decision support tools to analyze and visualize the data. Data request can be customized by spatial and temporal extend and analysis can be performed on custom subsets of the available data.

Behind the scenes

We will routinely perform evaluations of the usefulness of the information and tools provided. We will look at the effectiveness of the mechanisms for access, dissemination, summarization and visualization. User feedback as well as internal and external expert advice will guide improvements and enhancements to the SCENIC.
